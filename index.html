about.

I am a recent graduate of the Web Development Immersive course at General Asssembly - London. Although me of all people would have been the last to say I’d grow up to love this discipline, learning to code has been the most rewarding challenge of my life . Two things that have persistently interested me throughout my life are understanding how things work and creating things. This first manifested as a love for art, then psychology, and now coding; all of which adding value to the next. Tackling logic problems using a medium that allows me to build and create things is what I have found to love in learning the languages of modern technology.

Knowing that I enjoy a fast-paced learning environment, I am eager to learn more and look foward to securing my first role in the world of development. I would love to work in a medium to large team who I can learn from and stretch my abilities.

projects.

Off The Rail

Duration: 1 week

This was my final project during my Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly. It's a one-page MERN stack e-commerce/social media platform. Similar to the app 'Depop', users are able to buy and sell items, follow other users, and 'save for later' the items they like. More specifically, it is for buying and selling designer items at reduced prices and therefore has a large focus on how much you are saving. I used a maps API to integrate the sellers locations, and built an instant messaging component so that users can discuss sales and purchases.

JavaScript | React.js | HTML5 | SCSS | Node.js | MongoDB | Express

Duration: 1 week

This was a paired project. Jumee and I created xhbit - a review site for exhibitions. It is a fully restful MEAN stack web application. The site has a collection of galleries and lists all of the various exhibtions that are currently going on at each.
